# 1.1 Cost Analysis

App: `app/1.1cost-analysis/` · Data: CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners (`medicare-ProviderSummary.csv`)

---

## What we're doing

We analyze **how much Medicare spends** and **how much providers over-bill** — broken down by **state**, **provider specialty**, and **year**.

The goal is to find states and specialties where providers claim far more than Medicare allows, and to separate **total charges** from **drug-only charges**.

**Example:** In Maryland 2019, Internal Medicine providers billed **$7.1M** but Medicare only allowed **$3.1M** — **$4.0M in rejected over-charges**. Most of that gap is non-drug services; drug over-billing adds another **$68K**.

**One row in the app** = one year + state + provider type (e.g. 2019 · Maryland · Internal Medicine).

---

## What you see

| View | Shows |
|------|-------|
| **KPI cards (header)** | Portfolio-wide rejected over-charges + the state with highest Medicare spend |
| **Chart (top)** | Top 10 states by **Total Paid** — columns for claimed, allowed, paid, and drug amounts |
| **Table (bottom)** | Full financial breakdown per year × state × provider type |

**Filters:** Year, State, Provider Type

**Example — reading the chart:**

For California you might see five bars per state showing the money flow:
- **Total Charges Claimed** — what providers billed
- **Total Approved Cap** — what Medicare's fee schedule allows
- **Total Amount Paid** — what Medicare actually paid out
- **Drug Charges Claimed** / **Drug Amount Paid** — drug subset of the above

The gap between Claimed and Approved is the over-billing signal. The gap between Approved and Paid reflects deductibles, co-insurance, and other payment rules.

---

## Row grain

| Dimension | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Year** | 2019, 2020, … |
| **State** | MD → Maryland |
| **Provider Type** | Internal Medicine, Family Practice, Hematology-Oncology, … |

All providers in the same state + specialty + year are summed into one row.

Current dataset: **9,702 rows**.

---

## Columns

| Column | What it means | How it's calculated |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Year** | Calendar year of the data | From CMS data |
| **State** | Two-letter state code | From provider address |
| **Provider Type** | Medical specialty | From CMS provider classification |
| **Provider Count** | Unique providers in this bucket | Count of NPIs |
| **Total Beneficiaries** | Medicare patients served | Sum of beneficiaries |
| **Total Charges Claimed** | Gross amount providers billed | Sum of `Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg` |
| **Total Approved Cap** | What Medicare's fee schedule allows | Sum of `Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt` |
| **Rejected Over-Charges** | Billed amount Medicare disallowed | Total Claimed − Total Allowed |
| **Total Amount Paid** | Cash Medicare actually disbursed | Sum of `Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` |
| **Drug Charges Claimed** | Drug charges billed | Sum of `Drug_Sbmtd_Chrg` |
| **Rejected Drug Over-Charges** | Drug charges Medicare disallowed | Drug Claimed − Drug Allowed |
| **Drug Amount Paid** | Cash Medicare paid for drugs | Sum of `Drug_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` |

---

## Examples — how each column is built

### From one provider to one app row

Raw CMS provider row (Cumberland, MD — Internal Medicine, 2019):

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| NPI | 1003000126 |
| State | MD |
| Provider Type | Internal Medicine |
| Beneficiaries | 610 |
| Total Submitted | $519,136 |
| Total Allowed | $156,626.32 |
| Total Paid | $124,877.67 |
| Drug Submitted | $0 |

This provider's rejected charges:

```
$519,136 − $156,626.32 = $362,509.68 rejected
```

The app sums **28 Internal Medicine providers in MD for 2019** into one row.

---

### Total Charges Claimed

```
MD Internal Medicine 2019 = $7,121,347.63
```

Sum of every provider's `Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg` in that bucket.

---

### Total Approved Cap

```
MD Internal Medicine 2019 = $3,131,794.09
```

Medicare's fee schedule cap — the maximum Medicare considers reasonable.

---

### Rejected Over-Charges

```
Rejected = Total Claimed − Total Allowed
         = $7,121,347.63 − $3,131,794.09
         = $3,989,553.54
```

Roughly **56%** of what MD Internal Medicine providers billed was above the Medicare fee cap.

This is the primary audit signal: providers asked for $4.0M more than Medicare allows.

---

### Total Amount Paid

```
MD Internal Medicine 2019 = $2,397,904.96
```

Actual cash Medicare sent out — always **less than** the approved cap (patient cost-sharing, sequestration, etc.).

| Column | MD Internal Medicine 2019 | What it tells you |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Total Charges Claimed | **$7.1M** | What providers billed |
| Total Approved Cap | **$3.1M** | What Medicare allows |
| Rejected Over-Charges | **$4.0M** | The over-billing gap |
| Total Amount Paid | **$2.4M** | What Medicare actually paid |

---

### Drug columns

Same logic, but only for prescription drug charges:

```
Drug Claimed  = $164,644.10
Drug Allowed  = $96,353.44
Drug Rejected = $164,644.10 − $96,353.44 = $68,290.66
Drug Paid     = $92,087.97
```

For this bucket, drug over-billing is a small slice of the total rejection ($68K of $4.0M).

## Source data

Each CSV row = one provider × year. We aggregate by year + state + provider type.

| CMS field | Used for |
|-----------|----------|
| `Year` | Time dimension |
| `Rndrng_NPI` | Provider count |
| `Rndrng_Prvdr_State_Abrvtn` | State code |
| `Rndrng_Prvdr_Type` | Provider specialty |
| `Tot_Benes` | Beneficiary count |
| `Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg` | Total charges claimed |
| `Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt` | Total approved cap |
| `Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` | Total amount paid |
| `Drug_Sbmtd_Chrg` | Drug charges claimed |
| `Drug_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt` | Drug approved cap |
| `Drug_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` | Drug amount paid |

State full names (e.g. MD → Maryland) come from `medicare-StateReference.csv`.
