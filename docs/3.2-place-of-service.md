# 3.2 Place of Service Analysis

App: `app/3.2place-of-service/` · Data: CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners (`medicare-ServiceDetails.csv`)

Part of **Task 3 — Association Analysis**. Compares **Medicare payment per service unit** across **place-of-service settings** — **Facility (Hospital/ASC)** vs **Office (Non-Facility)** — at specialty and provider grain.

---

## What we're doing

We ask: *"Within a specialty, does Medicare pay more per service when the provider bills in a hospital/ASC vs an office — and which named providers drive the gap?"*

```
ServiceDetails (HCPCS × NPI grain)
         │
         ├── PlaceOfServiceAnalysis     → Year × Specialty × POS (chart)
         └── PlaceOfServiceProviderProfiles → Year × NPI × Specialty × POS (table)
```

**CMS flag:** `Place_Of_Srvc` → **F** = Facility (Hospital/ASC) · **O** = Office (Non-Facility)

**Example (2022 · Pain Management):** Office settings average **$309 per service** across **149,352** units vs Facility at only **$98 per service** on **119** units — but **PIZZO-BERKEY alone** bills **111,227 office services** at **$401/service**, accounting for **~74%** of all Pain Management office volume in the sample.

**One table row** = one **Year × NPI × Specialty × Place of Service** bucket (e.g. 2022 · PIZZO-BERKEY · Pain Management · Office).

**Example — reading the chart:**

Each specialty column is split into **Facility** vs **Office** stacked segments. Segment height = **Avg Payment per Service** (`TotalActualPayments ÷ TotalServicesRendered`) for that specialty × POS combination.

**Expected pattern:** Surgical specialties (e.g. **Ambulatory Surgical Center**) show taller **Facility** segments — hospital/ASC rates exceed office rates per procedure unit.

**Audit pattern:** A specialty where **Office** segment is unexpectedly tall, or one provider dominates office volume at a premium per-service rate — expand the table group to name the NPI.

**Tip:** Filter to a **single year** and one **specialty** before comparing POS segments. Defaults to **2022**.

## How the math works

### Step 1 — Source (`ServiceDetails`)

Each row = one provider × year × **HCPCS code** with CMS place-of-service flag:

```
Place_Of_Srvc = 'F'  →  Facility (Hospital/ASC)
Place_Of_Srvc = 'O'  →  Office (Non-Facility)
```

Dollar totals use the CMS line-item pattern (same as Task 1.2):

```
TotalActualPayments   = sum(Avg_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt × Tot_Srvcs)
TotalSubmittedCharges = sum(Avg_Sbmtd_Chrg × Tot_Srvcs)
TotalServicesRendered = sum(Tot_Srvcs)
```

### Step 2 — Specialty × POS aggregate (`PlaceOfServiceAnalysis`)

One row per **Year × Specialty × Place of Service**:

```
AvgPaymentPerService   = TotalActualPayments ÷ TotalServicesRendered
AvgSubmittedPerService = TotalSubmittedCharges ÷ TotalServicesRendered
TotalUniqueProviders   = count(distinct NPI)
```

### Step 3 — Provider drill-down (`PlaceOfServiceProviderProfiles`)

One row per **Year × NPI × Specialty × Place of Service** — powers the ALP table with `GroupBy: [Specialty]`.

## Case study — Pain Management: Facility vs Office (2022)

**Specialty aggregate** (`PlaceOfServiceAnalysis`):

| Place of Service | Providers | Services | Avg Pay/Service | Avg Submitted/Service | Total Paid |
|------------------|----------:|---------:|----------------:|----------------------:|-----------:|
| **Office** | 5 | **149,352** | **$309** | $462 | **$46.1M** |
| **Facility** | 3 | 119 | **$98** | $1,344 | $11.6K |

**Read:** Pain Management Medicare volume lives almost entirely in **Office** settings — **149K vs 119** service units. Facility per-service **submitted** charges look high ($1,344) but **payments** are low ($98) on tiny volume — likely a different procedure mix (few inpatient-style lines).

## Case study — PIZZO-BERKEY vs Pain Management peers (2022)

**Expanded Pain Management table** (sorted by Avg Payment per Service):

| Provider | POS | Avg Pay/Service | Services | Read |

| **PIZZO-BERKEY** | Office | **$401** | **111,227** | Dominates office volume · highest per-service pay |

| SUNDARARAJAN | Office | $42 | 17,536 | High volume · low per-service pay |
| BAQAI | Office | $80 | 672 | Moderate office billing |

**Read:** PIZZO is the **only** Pain Management provider with six-figure service volume **and** the highest per-service payment rate. Peers billing office settings (SUNDARARAJAN, BAQAI) pay **$42–$80/service** — PIZZO at **$401** is **5–10×** peer office intensity on a per-unit basis. This is the service-grain explanation behind Task 3.1's **$9,953/patient** and Task 2.1's **24 services/patient**.

## Worked example — PIZZO-BERKEY office row (2022)

**Target:** 2022 · NPI 1003056821 · Pain Management · Office

**Step 1 — Sum service lines** (all HCPCS with `Place_Of_Srvc = O`):

```
TotalServicesRendered  = 111,227
TotalActualPayments    = $44,568,818
TotalSubmittedCharges  = $61,375,758
```

**Step 2 — Compute averages**

```
AvgPaymentPerService   = 44,568,818 ÷ 111,227 = $401
AvgSubmittedPerService = 61,375,758 ÷ 111,227 = $552
```

**Step 3 — Compare to specialty office benchmark**

```
Pain Management office specialty avg = $309/service
PIZZO premium                        = (401 − 309) ÷ 309 = +30%
```

**Step 4 — Cross-check Task 1.1**

```
PIZZO ProviderSummary paid           = $44,579,940  ≈  POS TotalActualPayments
```

The POS row reconciles to the provider summary row that triggered Task 1.1 Row 1.

## Known data caveats

| Caveat | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Sample size** | 50,000-row CMS sample — not national Medicare |
| **POS mapping** | Only **F** and **O** mapped; other CMS POS codes → `Unknown Place of Service` |
| **Blended HCPCS** | Avg Payment per Service averages across all procedure types in the bucket |
| **Double rows** | Providers billing both F and O appear twice when specialty group is expanded |
| **Tiny facility panels** | Facility rows with < 100 services (e.g. Pain Management **119** units) produce volatile averages |
