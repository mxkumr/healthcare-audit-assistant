# 1.3 Cost-Complexity Frontier

App: `app/1.3behavioral-helath-risk/` · Data: CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners (`medicare-ProviderSummary.csv`)

Part of **Task 1 — Geographic & Complexity Analysis**. Evaluates whether Medicare **paid per beneficiary** is clinically justified by **patient complexity**, using behavioral-health (BH) burden to split each peer group into low vs high BH halves.

---

## What we're doing

We ask: *within the same state and specialty, do providers treating panels with higher behavioral-health comorbidity also show higher risk scores and higher Medicare spend?*

```
BH Burden (11 conditions) ──► Peer split A vs B ──► Compare Risk Score vs Paid/Bene
         │                              │                      │
    vs peer median              Group A / Group B      Zone 1 = expected slope
                                                         Zone 2 = audit signal
```

**Group A (Low BH Burden)** = providers whose average BH prevalence is **below** the peer-group median.  
**Group B (High BH Burden)** = providers at or **above** the peer median.

**One row in the app** = one **Year × State × Specialty × BH Burden Group** bucket (e.g. 2022 · PA · General Surgery · B - High BH Burden).

This is a **screening layer** — it aggregates peer groups, not individual NPIs. Flagged slices hand off to **Task 2.1 Provider Classification** for named-provider drill-down.

**Example — reading the chart:**

**Expected pattern (Zone 1):** Group **B** bubbles sit **right and above** Group **A** — higher BH burden → higher risk → higher paid/bene.

**Tip:** Filter to a **single year** and one **state** before comparing specialties. Peer medians are computed within Year × State × Specialty only.

## The classification logic

Each provider is scored once, then aggregated into A or B buckets. There are **no fixed dollar thresholds** for group assignment — only a **peer-relative median** and a **minimum group size**.

### Step 1 — Behavioral health burden (per provider)

**What it is:** Average prevalence across **11 CMS behavioral-health condition fields** on the provider's beneficiary panel.

**Formula:**

```
BH Burden = ( ADHD + Alcohol/Drug + Tobacco + Dementia + Anxiety + Bipolar
            + Mood + Depression + PD + PTSD + Schizophrenia ) ÷ 11
```

Null or suppressed CMS cells are treated as **0** (see caveats below).

**CMS fields used:**

| Field | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| `Bene_CC_BH_ADHD_OthCD_V1_Pct` | ADHD / other conduct disorder |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Alcohol_Drug_V1_Pct` | Alcohol / substance use |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Tobacco_V1_Pct` | Tobacco use |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Alz_NonAlzdem_V2_Pct` | Alzheimer's / dementia |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Anxiety_V1_Pct` | Anxiety |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Bipolar_V1_Pct` | Bipolar disorder |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Mood_V2_Pct` | Mood disorders |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Depress_V1_Pct` | Depression |
| `Bene_CC_BH_PD_V1_Pct` | Personality disorder |
| `Bene_CC_BH_PTSD_V1_Pct` | PTSD |
| `Bene_CC_BH_Schizo_OthPsy_V1_Pct` | Schizophrenia / other psychosis |

Each value is a **percentage of that provider's panel** with the condition (0–100).

---

### Step 2 — Peer median (per Year × State × Specialty)

**What it is:** The **median** BH burden across all providers in the same state, specialty, and year.

**Gate:**

| Rule | Value | Why |
|------|-------|-----|
| Minimum peer group size | **≥ 10 providers** | Median comparison is unstable below this; smaller groups are excluded entirely |

**View:** `BHBurdenMedianByGroup`

---

### Step 3 — Group assignment (A vs B)

**What it is:** Each provider is labeled relative to **their** peer median — not a national cutoff.

| Label | Rule |
|-------|------|
| **A - Low BH Burden** | Provider BH burden **<** peer `MedianBHBurden` |
| **B - High BH Burden** | Provider BH burden **≥** peer `MedianBHBurden` |

**View:** `BehavioralHealthRiskProfile` (inner join to median groups only)

---

### Step 4 — Aggregated measures (what the app displays)

| Measure | Formula | Chart role |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Avg Risk Score** | `round(avg(Bene_Avg_Risk_Scre), 3)` | **X-axis** — HCC complexity (`1.0` = national-average-cost patient) |
| **Paid per Beneficiary** | `round(sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt) ÷ sum(Tot_Benes), 3)` | **Y-axis** — ratio of sums, not average of ratios |
| **Provider Count** | `count(distinct Rndrng_NPI)` | Bubble size |
| **Total Medicare Paid** | `sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt)` | Dollar volume context |

---

### Reading the frontier together

| Pattern | Risk score (X) | Paid/bene (Y) | A vs B | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|---------------|--------|----------------|
| **Zone 1 — Normal** | B > A | B > A | Expected slope | Higher BH panels justify higher spend |
| **Zone 2 — Audit signal** | Low | High | A high or B low on Y | Spend not explained by complexity |
| **Inverted A/B** | B > A | A > B | Y-axis flipped | Same specialty/state — investigate low-BH half |

## Case study — PA General Surgery (Zone 1, expected pattern)

**2022 · Pennsylvania · General Surgery** — both A and B halves present (7 providers each).

| BH Group | Providers | Avg Risk | Paid/Bene | Total Paid |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **A - Low BH** | 7 | **1.435** | **$316** | $238,932 |
| **B - High BH** | 7 | **1.956** | **$1,072** | $1,273,015 |

**Read:** Group B has ~36% higher risk score and ~3.4× higher paid/beneficiary. Higher BH burden aligns with sicker panels and higher Medicare reimbursement — clinically plausible, not a fraud signal.

On the bubble chart, the **B** bubble sits **right and above** the **A** bubble. This is the **Zone 1 baseline** the frontier is designed to confirm.

---

## Case study — AL Nurse Practitioners (Zone 2, inverted pattern)

**2022 · Alabama · Nurse Practitioner** — 17 providers in each half.

| BH Group | Providers | Avg Risk | Paid/Bene | Total Paid |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **A - Low BH** | 17 | **1.222** | **$166** | $203,674 |
| **B - High BH** | 17 | **1.803** | **$151** | $292,837 |

**Read:** Group **B** has clearly higher risk (1.80 vs 1.22) but **lower** paid/beneficiary ($151 vs $166). Group **A** is the screening hit: lower-complexity half with **higher** payment intensity within the same AL NP peer group.

**Audit takeaway:** This does not prove fraud — it flags the slice for **Task 2.1** drill-down on individual NPIs. Possible explanations include panel mix, billing patterns, or statutory reimbursement differences within the low-BH half.

## Case study — CA Clinical Psychologists (high-cost outlier)

**2022 · California · Psychologist, Clinical**

| BH Group | Providers | Avg Risk | Paid/Bene | Beneficiaries |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **A - Low BH** | 6 | **1.096** | **$1,780** | 185 |
| **B - High BH** | 8 | **1.833** | **$676** | 909 |

**Read:** Group **A** ranks among the highest paid/bene rows in the dataset (~$1,780/patient) with a risk score only slightly above national average (1.10). Group **B** has nearly double the risk but less than half the per-beneficiary payment.

**Audit takeaway:** The table sort (paid/bene desc) surfaces Group **A** first — classic **Zone 2** screening. Confirm whether high payment reflects psychotherapy billing patterns, panel size (185 vs 909), or outlier providers before escalation.

## Worked example — building one peer-group row

**Target:** 2022 · TX · Internal Medicine · **B - High BH Burden**

**Step 1 — Find eligible peers**

All Internal Medicine providers in TX with ≥ 10 NPIs in 2022 enter the median pool.

**Step 2 — Compute median BH burden**

For each provider, average the 11 BH prevalence fields. Take the **median** across all TX IM providers → `MedianBHBurden`.

**Step 3 — Assign each provider**

Providers with BH burden ≥ median → **Group B**; below → **Group A**.

**Step 4 — Aggregate Group B rows**

```
AvgRiskScore       = avg(Bene_Avg_Risk_Scre) across Group B providers  → 2.036
PaidPerBeneficiary = sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt) ÷ sum(Tot_Benes)           → $407
ProviderCount      = count(distinct Rndrng_NPI)                         → 23
```

**Step 5 — Compare to Group A in same peer group**

TX IM Group A: risk **1.243**, paid/bene **$356** → B is right **and** above A → **Zone 1**.

## Known data caveats

| Caveat | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Peer group minimum** | Groups with fewer than **10 providers** in a Year × State × Specialty are **excluded** — no row appears for them |
| **Peer-relative only** | Group A in AL is not comparable to Group A in CA — always read within the same state and specialty |
| **BH suppression** | CMS suppresses prevalence values once a condition hits the **75% reporting threshold**; empty cells count as **0**, which may understate BH burden |
| **Aggregated grain** | One row blends many providers — Zone 2 at group level means *investigate this slice*, not *refer every NPI* |
| **Avg risk vs paid/bene** | `AvgRiskScore` is mean of provider means; `PaidPerBeneficiary` is ratio of sums — both are standard screening approximations |

## Source data

Each CSV row = one provider × year. Classifications are computed in `BHBurdenMedianByGroup` + `BehavioralHealthRiskProfile` — no manual tagging.

| CMS field | Used for |
|-----------|----------|
| `Year` | Time dimension |
| `Rndrng_NPI` | Provider key (aggregated to counts) |
| `Rndrng_Prvdr_Type` | Specialty / peer-group dimension |
| `Rndrng_Prvdr_State_Abrvtn` | State / peer-group dimension |
| `Bene_Avg_Risk_Scre` | HCC risk score — chart X-axis |
| `Tot_Benes` | Beneficiaries; divisor for paid/bene |
| `Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` | Medicare paid; numerator for paid/bene |
| `Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg` / `Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt` | Behind-the-scenes charge context |
| `Drug_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` | Part D spend |
| `Tot_HCPCS_Cds` | Procedure diversity |
| `Bene_CC_BH_*` (11 fields) | Behavioral health burden average |
