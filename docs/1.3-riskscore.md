# Task 1.3 — Risk Score Distribution: Cost-Complexity Frontier

## Purpose

The Cost-Complexity Frontier evaluates whether Medicare spending is clinically
justified by patient complexity.

The visualization compares provider peer groups using:

- **X-axis:** Average Beneficiary Risk Score
- **Y-axis:** Medicare Paid per Beneficiary

Behavioral health burden is used to separate providers into Low BH Burden
(Group A) and High BH Burden (Group B) within each
(Year, State, ProviderType) peer group.

## Zone 1 — Normal Clinical Baseline

The expected pattern is that **Group B (High BH Burden)** appears further to
the right and higher than **Group A (Low BH Burden)**.

This demonstrates that providers treating patients with greater behavioral
health comorbidity also treat more clinically complex patients and therefore
receive appropriately higher Medicare reimbursement.

Expected relationship:

> Higher Behavioral Health Burden → Higher Risk Score → Higher Cost

## Zone 2 — Audit / Fraud Detection

Provider groups appearing in the upper-left region of the chart represent an
anomalous pattern:

- Low Average Risk Score
- High Medicare Paid per Beneficiary

Because payment is not accompanied by higher patient complexity, these groups
become potential audit candidates.

These aggregated results are intended as a screening layer and hand off to
**Task 2.1 – Provider Cost Efficiency**, where individual providers can be
investigated in detail.

## Known Data Caveats

- Peer groups are only created when at least **10 providers** exist within a
  (Year, State, ProviderType) combination. Smaller groups are excluded to
  avoid unstable comparisons.
- Behavioral Health Burden is calculated as the composite average of **11
  behavioral health condition prevalence percentages**.
- CMS suppresses prevalence values once a condition reaches the **75% reporting
  threshold**. Suppressed values appear as empty cells and may slightly reduce
  the calculated behavioral health burden for affected providers.
