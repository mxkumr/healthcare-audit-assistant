# 1.3 — Behavioral Health Risk Profile (Risk Score Analysis)

## Purpose

This feature explores **distributions of beneficiary risk scores** to understand
differences in patient complexity across providers and regions. It answers two
related questions:

1. Within a given state and specialty, which providers are treating a
   disproportionately high share of patients with behavioral health (BH)
   conditions — and how does that correlate with overall patient risk score?
2. How does average patient risk score vary by state?

## Data Model

### `BHBurdenMedianByGroup` (helper view)

For every `(Year, State, ProviderType)` peer group with **10 or more providers**,
this view calculates the **median behavioral health burden** — the average of 11
BH-related comorbidity prevalence percentages (ADHD, alcohol/drug, tobacco,
Alzheimer's/dementia, anxiety, bipolar, mood disorders, depression, personality
disorder, PTSD, schizophrenia/other psychosis) across all providers in that peer
group.

Groups with fewer than 10 providers are excluded (`having count(...) >= 10`) so
that a "median" isn't computed from a statistically unreliable sample.

### `BehavioralHealthRiskProfile` (main view)

Each individual provider is compared against their peer group's median BH
burden (calculated above) and classified into one of two groups:

- **A — Low BH Burden**: provider's own BH burden score is below the peer
  group median
- **B — High BH Burden**: provider's own BH burden score is at or above the
  peer group median

Providers are then aggregated into one row per
`(Year, State, ProviderType, BHBurdenGroup)` combination, producing summary
metrics for each group:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `ProviderCount` | Number of providers in this group |
| `AvgRiskScore` | Average CMS beneficiary risk score across this group's providers, rounded to 3 decimal places |
| `PaidPerBeneficiary` | Total Medicare paid ÷ total beneficiaries, rounded to 3 decimal places |
| `TotalSubmitted`, `TotalAllowed`, `TotalPaid`, `TotalBeneficiaries`, `TotalDrugPaid` | Aggregate financial/utilization totals |
| `AvgUniqueProcedures` | Average distinct HCPCS codes billed per provider |
| `MedicareAcceptCount` | Count of providers who accept Medicare assignment |

### Why the same state appears in multiple rows

**This is expected, not a data quality issue.** The grain of this view is
`State + ProviderType + BHBurdenGroup`, not `State` alone. A state will have
one row per specialty per burden group present in its data.

Example — Alabama, Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetists (CRNA):

| State | ProviderType | BHBurdenGroup | ProviderCount | AvgRiskScore | PaidPerBeneficiary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AL | CRNA | A - Low BH Burden | 7 | 1.697 | 105.348 |
| AL | CRNA | B - High BH Burden | 9 | 2.044 | 108.499 |

There are 16 total CRNA providers in Alabama. Each provider individually
belongs to **exactly one** group — 7 fell below the peer median and were
grouped into the "A" row, 9 fell at/above it and were grouped into the "B"
row. No provider is counted twice; the two rows represent two disjoint
subsets of the same 16 providers.

This structure is intentional: it lets the same state/specialty be compared
side-by-side across burden groups (e.g. AL CRNAs in the high-burden group
have a notably higher average risk score — 2.044 vs 1.697 — supporting the
hypothesis that behavioral health burden correlates with overall patient
complexity).

## Decimal Precision

`AvgRiskScore` and `PaidPerBeneficiary` are explicitly rounded at the
database level using `round(..., 3)` combined with `cast(... as Decimal(10,3))`,
rather than relying on the CDS type annotation alone (`: Decimal(10,3)` on its
own only affects OData metadata, not the underlying computed value).

## Fiori Elements Configuration

- **Entity**: exposed as `@readonly` projection `BehavioralHealthRiskProfile`
  on `MedicareService`.
- **Aggregation**: `Aggregation.ApplySupported`, `Analytics.AggregatedProperties`,
  and per-property `Analytics.Dimension` / `Analytics.Measure` annotations
  enable the Analytical List Page (ALP) to group and aggregate rows.
- **Table**: `AnalyticalTable` (required for collapsible `State: XX ▾` group
  headers — `GridTable` and `ResponsiveTable` do not support the same grouped
  collapse behavior for this scenario).
- **Chart**: `UI.Chart` — Bar chart, dimension `State`, measure `AvgRiskScore`.

### Known limitation — HeatMap chart type

The original design called for a `#HeatMap` chart (State × BHBurdenGroup,
colored by AvgRiskScore). Despite correct annotations
(`DimensionAttributes` with `#Category`/`#Category2` roles, `MeasureAttributes`
with `#Axis1`), the HeatMap and Bubble chart types did not render in this
environment — the chart pane consistently returned an empty state even
though the underlying `$apply` OData query returned correct data when tested
directly.

A single-dimension `#Bar` chart (proven reliable in this project's other
entities, e.g. `RiskScoreDistribution`, `CostByStateProviderType`) was used as
the working substitute. The full State × BHBurdenGroup breakdown remains
available in the table below the chart.

**Follow-up (not yet resolved):** the Bar chart currently displays a single
aggregated bar rather than one bar per state by default; selecting `State`
via the chart's **View By** control produces the expected per-state
breakdown, but this is a manual step rather than the default view. Revisiting
the `PresentationVariant`/chart `$apply` binding, or evaluating a coarser
state-only aggregate view, is recommended as a follow-up.
