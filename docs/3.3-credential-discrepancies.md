# 3.3 Credential Discrepancies

App: `app/3.3credential-discrepancies/` · Data: CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners (`medicare-ProviderSummary.csv`)

Part of **Task 3 - Association Analysis**. Decomposes the billing gap between what different **clinical credential classes** submit to Medicare versus what Medicare pays - especially **physicians (MD/DO)** vs **mid-level providers (NP/PA)**.

---

## What we're doing

We analyze the **structural financial gap** between practitioner classes and split it into two distinct audit stories:

```
Submitted ──────► Allowed ──────► Paid
    │                 │              │
    │  Charge Padding │ Policy Gap   │
    │  (fraud signal) │ (statutory)  │
```

**Charge padding** = billing above Medicare fee-schedule caps (`Submitted - Allowed`).  
**Policy shortfall** = allowed amount not realized as payment (`Allowed - Paid`) - includes the statutory **85% NP/PA reimbursement rule** and patient cost-sharing.

**One row in the app** = one **Year × Standardized Credential** bucket (e.g. 2022 · NP - Nurse Practitioner).

## Columns

### Table columns

| Column | What it means | How it's calculated | Auditor note |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Standardized Credential** | Clinical practitioner class after normalizing messy CMS credential text | `CASE` on `Rndrng_Prvdr_Crdntls` → MD, DO, NP, PA, CRNA, Unspecified, Other | Row identifier - compare behavior **across** classes, not individual providers |
| **Total Unique Providers** | How many distinct providers (NPIs) belong to this bucket in the selected year | `count(distinct Rndrng_NPI)` | Scale context - same write-off rate on 5,000 NPIs vs 50 matters for triage |
| **Charge Padding Amount** | Total dollars billed **above** what Medicare's fee schedule allows | `sum(Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg) - sum(Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt)` | **Fraud signal** - charge inflation (e.g. asking $1,000 when cap is $100). Table sorts by this column descending |
| **Charge Padding Rate (%)** | Normalized charge inflation - share of submitted bill rejected by fee schedule | `round(ChargePaddingAmt ÷ TotalSubmittedCharges × 100)` | Pairs with dollar amount: intensity vs volume. Matches the top chart |
| **Policy Shortfall Amount** | Total dollars between the **allowed** cap and **actual** payment | `sum(Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt) - sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt)` | **Policy gap** - 85% NP/PA rule, deductibles, coinsurance. High here + ~85% Paid/Allowed = often statutory, not abuse |
| **Paid-to-Allowed Rate (%)** | What share of the fee-schedule allowance Medicare actually paid out | `round(TotalActualPayments ÷ TotalAllowedCharges × 100)` | NP/PA should cluster near **~85%**; MD/DO typically higher. Proves policy artifact when charge padding is moderate |

### Chart measure (top layer)

The chart uses the same **Charge Padding Rate (%)** column shown in the table - compare bar heights across credentials at a glance.

### Filter fields (not in table)

| Field | What it means | Auditor note |
|-------|---------------|--------------|
| **Year** | Calendar year of CMS provider summary data | Always filter to **one year** before comparing rows - defaults to **2022** |
| **Standardized Credential** | Dropdown of all seven credential buckets | Select a single class (e.g. NP only) to isolate mid-level policy effects |

### Behind-the-scenes measures (in CDS, not shown in table)

| Measure | What it means | How it's calculated |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Total Submitted Charges** | Gross amount providers billed Medicare | `sum(Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg)` |
| **Total Allowed Charges** | Medicare fee-schedule cap | `sum(Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt)` |
| **Total Actual Payments** | Cash Medicare disbursed | `sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt)` |
| **Total Patients Served** | Beneficiaries attributed to providers in bucket | `sum(Tot_Benes)` |

**Identity check:** `ChargePaddingAmt + PolicyShortfallAmt = TotalSubmittedCharges - TotalActualPayments`

## Practical examples — column by column

Each example uses a **single credential row** (Year 2022). Dollar figures are illustrative; your app shows real aggregated CMS totals.

### Standardized Credential

**What you see:** `NP - Nurse Practitioner`

**Example:** The bucket rolls up every provider whose raw credential field matches `NP`, `N.P.`, or `NURSE PRACTITIONER`. You are **not** looking at one nurse practitioner — you are comparing **the entire NP class** against MD, PA, etc.

**Auditor takeaway:** Use this column to ask *"Does this practitioner class behave differently?"* — not *"Is this one provider fraudulent?"*

---

### Total Unique Providers

**What you see:** `3,482`

**Example:** In 2022, 3,482 distinct NPIs mapped to the NP bucket.

| Providers | Same charge padding rate | Triage priority |
|-----------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| **50 NPIs** at 75% padding | Small dollar volume | Monitor |
| **3,482 NPIs** at 75% padding | Large systemic exposure | **Escalate** |

**Auditor takeaway:** Rate tells you **intensity**; provider count tells you **blast radius**.

---

### Charge Padding Amount (Fraud Signal)

**Formula:** `Submitted - Allowed` (summed across all providers in the bucket)

**Micro example (one procedure logic):**

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Provider submits | **$1,000** |
| Medicare fee schedule allows | **$100** |
| Charge padding on this line | **$900** |

**Macro example (NP bucket, 2022 - illustrative):**

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Total submitted (all NPs) | $420M |
| Total allowed | $168M |
| **Charge padding amount** | **$252M** |

**Auditor takeaway:** Large dollar padding = **material overcharging vs Medicare caps**. This is the table's **sort column** — worst buckets float to the top. Overcharging signal ✅ · fraud proof ❌ (need line-item drill-down).

---

### Charge Padding Rate (%)

**Formula:** `Charge Padding Amount ÷ Total Submitted × 100`

**Same micro example:**

```
($1,000 - $100) ÷ $1,000 × 100 = 90%
```

**Interpretation guide:**

| Rate | Meaning | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| **20-40%** | Most charges near fee-schedule norms | Routine monitoring |
| **40-60%** | Noticeable markup above caps | Watch list |
| **60%+** | Majority of submitted bill exceeds Medicare caps | **Priority triage** |

**Example - two buckets, same rate, different scale:**

| Credential | Charge padding $ | Submitted $ | Rate | Read |
|------------|------------------|-------------|------|------|
| CRNA | $12M | $15M | **80%** | High intensity, smaller volume |
| MD | $800M | $1.0B | **80%** | Same intensity, **much** larger exposure |

**Auditor takeaway:** Higher rate = **more overcharging relative to submitted bill**. Pairs with Charge Padding Amount: *"How much?"* vs *"How aggressive?"* Matches the **top chart bar height**.

**Important:** High rate = strong **overcharging indicator**, not automatic fraud. Common billing practice in some specialties also inflates submitted charges before Medicare cuts them.

---

### Policy Shortfall Amount (Statutory Gap)

**Formula:** `Allowed - Paid` (summed across the bucket)

**Micro example:**

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Medicare allows | **$100** |
| Medicare actually pays | **$85** |
| Policy shortfall | **$15** |

That $15 gap is **not** charge padding — the fee schedule already accepted $100. The shortfall comes from **payment rules** (e.g. 85% mid-level rate) and **patient cost-sharing**.

**Macro example (NP bucket, 2022 - illustrative):**

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Total allowed | $168M |
| Total paid | $143M |
| **Policy shortfall** | **$25M** |

**Auditor takeaway:** High policy shortfall **with moderate charge padding** often means **statutory/policy effects**, not billing abuse. Compare NP/PA vs MD here only **after** checking Paid-to-Allowed.

---

### Paid-to-Allowed Rate (%) — NP/PA ~85%

**Formula:** `Total Paid ÷ Total Allowed × 100`

**Micro example - NP statutory rule:**

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Allowed | $100 |
| Paid (85% mid-level rate) | $85 |
| **Paid-to-allowed rate** | **85%** |

**Macro comparison (2022 - illustrative):**

| Credential | Paid / Allowed | Typical read |
|------------|----------------|--------------|
| **MD** | ~92% | Full physician reimbursement + normal cost-sharing |
| **NP** | ~85% | **Expected** — statutory 85% of physician rate |
| **PA** | ~84% | Same mid-level policy pattern |
| **MD at 70%** | ~70% | **Unusual** — investigate (not a normal NP policy story) |

**Auditor takeaway:** If NP/PA shows **~85%** here, the **85% Medicare mid-level rule** is visible in the data. That **proves policy artifact** when charge padding is moderate. If charge padding is **also** extreme, investigate both.

## Combined scenarios — reading all columns together

### Scenario A — Policy artifact (not fraud)

**Row:** `NP - Nurse Practitioner` · 2022

| Column | Example value | Read |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Total Unique Providers | 3,482 | Large class |
| Charge Padding Amount | $120M | Moderate |
| Charge Padding Rate | 45% | Elevated but not extreme |
| Policy Shortfall Amount | **$180M** | **High** |
| Paid-to-Allowed Rate | **~85%** | **Statutory NP rate** |

**Conclusion:** High policy shortfall + ~85% paid/allowed = **expected mid-level reimbursement policy**. Do **not** refer for fraud based on write-offs alone.

---

### Scenario B — Charge padding / overcharging (investigate)

**Row:** `MD - Doctor of Medicine` · 2022

| Column | Example value | Read |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Total Unique Providers | 12,000 | High exposure |
| Charge Padding Amount | **$800M** | **Top of sorted table** |
| Charge Padding Rate | **72%** | **Very high** |
| Policy Shortfall Amount | $200M | Normal for class |
| Paid-to-Allowed Rate | ~92% | Normal for physicians |

**Conclusion:** Extreme padding $ + high rate + **normal** paid/allowed = **charge inflation vs fee schedule** is the story, **not** payment policy. **Escalate** for specialty/provider drill-down.

---

### Scenario C — Both padding and abnormal payment (highest priority)

**Row:** `Other Specialists` · 2022

| Column | Example value | Read |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Charge Padding Rate | **78%** | Aggressive billing vs caps |
| Paid-to-Allowed Rate | **62%** | **Abnormally low** vs peers |
| Policy Shortfall Amount | Very high | Beyond typical cost-sharing |

**Conclusion:** High padding **and** paid/allowed far below peer credential classes → **highest triage priority**. Neither column alone tells the full story.

---

### Scenario D — Reading the chart + table together

**Chart:** NP bar = 45% padding rate · MD bar = 72%

**Table:** MD row shows $800M padding amount; NP row shows $120M

**Conclusion:** MD class has **both** higher intensity (taller bar) and **larger dollar exposure** (table sort). NP bar is shorter — but still check **Paid-to-Allowed ~85%** before dismissing NP write-offs as fraud.

## How credentials are standardized

Raw CMS field: `Rndrng_Prvdr_Crdntls` (free-text, inconsistent formatting).

| Raw examples | Standardized bucket |
|--------------|---------------------|
| `MD`, `M.D.`, `M.D., F.A.C.P.` | MD - Doctor of Medicine |
| `DO`, `D.O.` | DO - Osteopathic Medicine |
| `NP`, `N.P.`, `NURSE PRACTITIONER` | NP - Nurse Practitioner |
| `PA`, `P.A.`, `PHYSICIAN ASSISTANT` | PA - Physician Assistant |
| `CRNA` | CRNA - Nurse Anesthetist |
| blank / null | Unspecified Credentials |
| everything else | Other Specialists |

Implementation uses `upper()` + pattern matching in `db/schema.cds`. Aggregation **groups by the standardized CASE expression**, not the raw credential string - so `MD` and `M.D., F.A.C.P.` roll into one bucket.

## How the math works

### Step 1 - Roll up financial totals per credential bucket

```
TotalSubmittedCharges = sum(Tot_Sbmtd_Chrg)       ← what providers billed
TotalAllowedCharges   = sum(Tot_Mdcr_Alowd_Amt)  ← Medicare fee-schedule cap
TotalActualPayments   = sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt)   ← cash Medicare paid out
TotalUniqueProviders  = count(distinct Rndrng_NPI)
TotalPatientsServed   = sum(Tot_Benes)
```

### Step 2 - Split the gap (core of Task 3.3)

| Measure | Formula | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **ChargePaddingAmt** | Submitted - Allowed | Charge inflation above Medicare caps |
| **PolicyShortfallAmt** | Allowed - Paid | Payment rules + patient cost-sharing |
| **PaidToAllowedRatePct** | round((Paid ÷ Allowed) × 100) | Share of allowed amount actually paid |
| **ChargePaddingRatePct** | round((ChargePadding ÷ Submitted) × 100) | % of bill rejected by fee schedule *(chart)* |

**Identity check:**

```
ChargePaddingAmt + PolicyShortfallAmt = Submitted - Paid
```

The total write-off decomposes cleanly into charge inflation vs payment-policy gap.

## Dataset caveats

- This project uses a **50,000-row sample** of CMS Medicare data (2019–2023), not the full national file.
- Macro credential buckets **mix all specialties and geographies** - NP averages include primary care, psychiatry, etc.
- **Unspecified** and **Other Specialists** buckets may include organizations or mis-coded credentials - interpret cautiously.
- **Paid-to-Allowed ~85%** for NP/PA is an expected pattern, not a hard rule in the data - patient cost-sharing and service mix shift the rate.
- Charge padding at macro level does not prove individual provider fraud without HCPCS-level drill-down.
