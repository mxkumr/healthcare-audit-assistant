# 3.1 Risk-Cost-Volume Dynamics

App: `app/3.1risk-dynamics/` · Data: CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners (`medicare-ProviderSummary.csv`)

Part of **Task 3 — Association Analysis**. Compares **clinical complexity** (HCC risk score) against **Medicare spend per patient** at the **specialty** level — a macro screening layer before drilling to individual NPIs in Task 2.1.

---

## What we're doing

We ask: *"For each Medicare specialty, does billing intensity match the clinical complexity of its patient panel?"*

```
ProviderCostEfficiency (NPI grain)
         │
         ▼ group by Year × Specialty
RiskCostVolumeDynamics (96 rows in 2022)
         │
         ├── Left axis  → Avg Patient Risk Score (complexity)
         └── Right axis → Avg Cost per Patient (billing intensity)
```

**Task 2.1** classifies **individual providers** on fixed cost/utilization thresholds. **Task 3.1** rolls those providers up to **specialty × year** so the ALP chart shows **one dual-axis column pair per specialty** instead of thousands of overlapping NPI scatter points.

**Example (2022):** **Opioid Treatment Program** averages **$6,597 per patient** with a risk score of only **1.25** (~25% above national-average complexity). That is **12×** the unweighted cross-specialty mean cost (~$534/patient) without a proportionate complexity signal — the table sort surfaces it first.

**One row in the app** = one **Year × Specialty** bucket (e.g. 2022 · Pain Management).

This is a **screening layer** — it aggregates specialties, not individual NPIs. Flagged rows hand off to **Task 2.1 Provider Classification** for named-provider drill-down.

**Example — reading the chart:**

Each specialty gets **two columns** on the same category label. The **left bar** is beneficiary-weighted **clinical complexity** (HCC risk score). The **right bar** is beneficiary-weighted **Medicare spend per patient**.

**Expected pattern:** Both bars are tall — high-cost specialties serving genuinely complex panels (e.g. **Medical Oncology**: risk **1.97**, cost **$2,250/patient**).

**Audit pattern:** A **tall right bar** with a **short left bar** — high spend not explained by patient complexity (e.g. **Opioid Treatment Program**: risk **1.25**, cost **$6,597/patient**).

**Volume pattern:** High cost with **low risk** can also mean **utilization-driven** billing rather than acuity — see **Physical Therapist in Private Practice** (risk **1.07**, cost **$634/patient**).

**Tip:** Filter to a **single year** before comparing specialties. Defaults to **2022** (96 specialty rows).

## How the math works

### Step 1 — Source (`ProviderCostEfficiency`)

Each provider-year inherits per-patient metrics from Task 2.1:

```
Cost per Patient     = Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt ÷ Tot_Benes   (as CostPerBeneficiary)
Avg Risk Score       = Bene_Avg_Risk_Scre               (as AvgRiskScore)
Total Beneficiaries  = Tot_Benes                        (as TotalBeneficiaries)
```

### Step 2 — Specialty aggregation (`RiskCostVolumeDynamics`)

One row per **Year + Specialty** (`ProviderType` aliased as `Specialty`):

```
Total Unique Providers = count(distinct NPI)

PatientRiskScore =
  round( Σ(AvgRiskScore × TotalBeneficiaries) ÷ Σ(TotalBeneficiaries), 2 )
  // beneficiary-weighted mean — NOT a simple avg of provider means

CostPerPatient =
  round( Σ(CostPerBeneficiary × TotalBeneficiaries) ÷ Σ(TotalBeneficiaries), 2 )
  // equivalent to sum(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt) ÷ sum(Tot_Benes)

TotalPatientsServed    = sum(TotalBeneficiaries)
TotalActualPayments    = sum(CostPerBeneficiary × TotalBeneficiaries)
```

**Risk score context:** `1.0` = nationally average-cost patient. `2.0` ≈ twice as complex/expensive to treat as average. `3.84` (Nephrology) ≈ nearly 4× average complexity.

### Reading the frontier together

| Pattern | Risk score (left) | Cost/patient (right) | Interpretation |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Aligned — expected** | High | High | Complex specialty, expensive care (oncology, nephrology) |
| **Mismatch — audit signal** | Low | High | Spend not explained by panel complexity (opioid treatment, rheumatology) |
| **Volume-driven** | Low | High | Many visits/procedures, not sick panels (physical therapy) |
| **Low-intensity** | Low–moderate | Low | High-volume, low-margin (diagnostic radiology) |

## Case study — Opioid Treatment Program (cost-complexity mismatch)

**2022 · Opioid Treatment Program** — highest cost/patient in the dataset.

| Specialty | Providers | Avg Risk | Cost/Patient | Patients | Total Paid |
|-----------|----------:|---------:|-------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| **Opioid Treatment Program** | 5 | **1.25** | **$6,597** | 190 | $1.25M |

**Read:** Risk score is only ~25% above national average, yet per-patient spend is **12×** the unweighted cross-specialty mean (~$534). Only **5 providers** and **190 patients** — a small panel where a few high-billing NPIs can dominate the specialty average.

**Audit takeaway:** Classic **mismatch pattern** — tall right chart bar, short left bar. Does not prove fraud; flags the specialty for **Task 2.1** drill-down to identify which NPIs drive the $6,597 average.

## Case study — Pain Management vs Medical Oncology (similar complexity, different cost)

**2022** — both specialties serve ~2× average-complexity panels.

| Specialty | Providers | Avg Risk | Cost/Patient | Patients | Total Paid |
|-----------|----------:|---------:|-------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| **Pain Management** | 25 | **1.96** | **$3,733** | 13,273 | **$49.5M** |
| **Medical Oncology** | 30 | **1.97** | **$2,250** | 11,170 | $25.1M |

**Read:** Nearly identical risk scores (**1.96 vs 1.97**), but Pain Management bills **66% more per patient** and drives **double the total dollar volume** ($49.5M vs $25.1M). Oncology cost is high but proportionate to complexity; Pain Management sits **above** the expected cost-complexity slope.

**Audit takeaway:** Interventional procedures, infusion, and device billing may explain the gap — but this is a specialty-level pattern worth investigating before dismissing as clinical norm.

## Case study — Neprology vs Physical Therapy (complexity vs utilization)

**2022** — two different reasons a specialty can look "expensive."

| Specialty | Providers | Avg Risk | Cost/Patient | Patients | Total Paid |
|-----------|----------:|---------:|-------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| **Nephrology** | 67 | **3.84** | $429 | 26,352 | $11.3M |
| **Physical Therapist in Private Practice** | 593 | **1.07** | **$634** | 39,698 | $25.2M |

**Read — Neprology:** Highest major-specialty risk score (**3.84** — end-stage renal panels) with moderate cost ($429/patient). Chart shows a **tall left bar, moderate right bar** — complexity-aligned, not a billing outlier.

**Read — Physical Therapy:** **Lowest risk** among major specialties (1.07) but **$634/patient** — higher than Internal Medicine ($301). High visit volume per beneficiary drives cost without high HCC scores. This is the **volume** dimension in "Risk-Cost-**Volume** Dynamics."

## Case study — Internal Medicine vs Family Practice (expected clinical ordering)

**2022** — two high-volume primary-care specialties.

| Specialty | Providers | Avg Risk | Cost/Patient | Patients | Total Paid |
|-----------|----------:|---------:|-------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| **Internal Medicine** | 729 | **1.81** | **$301** | 180,439 | **$54.2M** |
| **Family Practice** | 676 | **1.30** | **$261** | 144,008 | $37.6M |

**Read:** IM panels are **39% more complex** (1.81 vs 1.30 risk) and **15% more expensive** ($301 vs $261/patient). That ordering is clinically sensible — internists see sicker patients. Both bars scale together → **aligned pattern**, not an audit signal.

**Audit takeaway:** Internal Medicine also has the **largest absolute dollar exposure** in the sample ($54.2M) — high volume matters even when per-patient cost is moderate.

## Worked example — building the Pain Management row (2022)

**Target:** 2022 · Pain Management

**Step 1 — Collect providers**

All providers where `Rndrng_Prvdr_Type = 'Pain Management'` in 2022 → **25 distinct NPIs**.

**Step 2 — Sum beneficiaries and payments**

```
Σ(TotalBeneficiaries) = 13,273 patients
Σ(Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt)  = $49,546,900
```

**Step 3 — Compute beneficiary-weighted cost**

```
CostPerPatient = 49,546,900 ÷ 13,273 = $3,732.91
```

**Step 4 — Compute beneficiary-weighted risk**

For each of the 25 providers, multiply `AvgRiskScore × TotalBeneficiaries`, sum, divide by 13,273:

```
PatientRiskScore = 1.96
```

**Step 5 — Compare to Medical Oncology (same year)**

Oncology: risk **1.97**, cost **$2,250** → nearly same complexity, **40% lower** cost/patient → Pain Management sits above the expected frontier line.

## 2.1 vs 1.3 vs 3.1 — when to use which

| Question | Use |
|----------|-----|
| "Is this provider above $900/patient?" | **2.1** fixed Cost Classification |
| "Within PA General Surgery, does high BH burden explain higher spend?" | **1.3** peer-group cost-complexity frontier |
| "Which specialties look expensive for their patient mix?" | **3.1** specialty dual-axis chart + table |
| "Which named NPI drives Pain Management's $3,733 average?" | **2.1** provider drill-down |
| "Do organizations bill more than individuals in this specialty?" | **2.2B** entity comparison |

**Audit caveat:** Specialty aggregates **hide mix effects** — 30 oncology NPIs are not interchangeable. A high-cost specialty row is a **triage signal**, not a fraud verdict. Small panels (**Home Infusion: 1 provider, 22 patients**) produce volatile averages.

## Known data caveats

| Caveat | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Sample size** | This project uses a **50,000-row sample** of CMS Medicare data (2019–2023), not the full national file |
| **Specialty grain on ALP** | Chart/table stay Year × Specialty; NPI list is on the **object page** (and Provider filter cascades via `providers`) |
| **Weighted vs unweighted** | `PatientRiskScore` and `CostPerPatient` are beneficiary-weighted; cross-specialty means in this doc are unweighted averages across 96 rows unless noted |
| **Small panels** | Specialties with few providers/patients (Opioid Treatment, Home Infusion) can show extreme per-patient figures |
| **Utilization not in table** | Services-per-patient was removed at specialty grain; high cost + low risk may still mean high visit volume — check Task 2.1 |

## Source data

Each CSV row = one provider × year. Aggregations are computed in `RiskCostVolumeDynamics` — no manual tagging.

| CMS field | Used for |
|-----------|----------|
| `Year` | Time dimension |
| `Rndrng_Prvdr_Type` | Specialty (group key) |
| `Rndrng_NPI` | Provider key (aggregated to counts) |
| `Bene_Avg_Risk_Scre` | HCC risk score — chart left axis |
| `Tot_Benes` | Beneficiaries; divisor for cost/patient |
| `Tot_Mdcr_Pymt_Amt` | Medicare paid; numerator for cost/patient |

Built on **`ProviderCostEfficiency`** (Task 2.1 view) — same per-provider formulas, rolled up to specialty.
